# 🔥 Observation of Gel Propellant Combustion Using the Schlieren Method

> Experimental investigation of gel propellant combustion using high-speed Schlieren imaging, image processing, and D²-law analysis.

---

## 📖 Overview

This project presents an experimental study on the combustion characteristics of an ethanol-based gel propellant using the **Schlieren optical visualization technique**. The objective was to observe the combustion process, analyze flame evolution, and determine the burning rate of the fuel droplet through image processing.

The experiment was carried out as part of **ME221** at **Indian Institute of Technology Indore**.

# ⚙ Experimental Procedure

The experiment followed these major steps:

1. Preparation of ethanol-based gel propellant droplet.
2. Alignment of the Z-type Schlieren optical system.
3. Ignition of the gel droplet.
4. High-speed recording of combustion.
5. Image processing for droplet extraction.
6. Measurement of droplet diameter.
7. D²-law analysis.
8. Burning rate constant calculation.

---

# 🔬 Fuel Used

* Ethanol-Based Gel Propellant
* Carbomer Gelling Agent
* Water

The gel exhibits non-Newtonian behavior and undergoes multiple combustion stages including swelling, disruptive burning, and carbonization.

# 📈 D²-Law Analysis

<img width="802" height="495" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-29 170114" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3c0b38c8-b771-475b-9ebf-9bbd8ae2135a" />

The squared droplet diameter was plotted against time to determine the burning rate constant.

The analysis confirmed a near-linear regression during the quasi-steady combustion phase, validating the applicability of the D²-law for the selected operating conditions.
